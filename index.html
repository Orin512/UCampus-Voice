🎓 About Campus Voice

Empowering students to shape a better campus since 2022.

Who We Are

Campus Voice is a student-led platform built to give every student a safe, structured, and meaningful channel to raise concerns about life on campus. From broken facilities to academic disputes, we believe every concern deserves to be heard — and acted upon.

Founded in 2022 by a small group of student government leaders, Campus Voice has since grown into the university's primary student feedback system, handling thousands of submissions per semester.

Our Mission

To bridge the gap between students and administration by creating a transparent, accountable system that ensures student concerns are tracked, addressed, and resolved in a timely manner.

How It Works

  • Students submit a concern using the form on the home page.
  • Our moderation team reviews each submission within 24 hours.
  • Concerns are forwarded to the appropriate department or office.
  • Status is updated as: Pending → In Progress → Resolved.
  • Anonymous submissions are fully protected and untraceable.

Our Team

We are a volunteer team of student moderators, one faculty advisor, and a technical team. We operate under the Student Government Association (SGA) and report directly to the Dean of Students Office.

  • Platform Director: Alex M. Cruz (SGA President)

🔒 Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2026

1. Information We Collect

When you submit a concern, we may collect your name, student ID, and email address — only if you choose not to submit anonymously. Anonymous submissions collect no personally identifiable information.

2. Anonymous Submissions

When you enable the "Submit Anonymously" toggle, your submission is processed without any identifying data. No IP address, device fingerprint, or session data is linked to your submission. Even our moderators cannot identify anonymous submitters.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To route your concern to the correct department or office.
  • To follow up with updates on your submission's status.
  • To generate anonymized aggregate statistics (e.g., how many concerns were submitted per category).
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties.

4. Data Storage & Security

All data is stored securely on university-managed servers within the Philippines, compliant with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173). Access is limited to authorized moderators and administrators.

5. Data Retention

Resolved complaints are archived for 2 years before permanent deletion. Anonymous submissions are deleted after 1 year. You may request deletion of your personally-submitted data at any time by contacting us.

6. Your Rights

  • Right to access your submitted data.
  • Right to correct inaccurate information.
  • Right to erasure of your personal data.
  • Right to file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission.

7. Cookies

This platform uses only functional localStorage (no tracking cookies) to save your submitted concerns locally in your browser for reference. No third-party analytics or advertising trackers are used.

8. Contact for Privacy Concerns

If you have questions about this policy, contact our Data Privacy Officer at [email protected] or visit the Office of Data Protection, Admin Building Room 104.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about submitting and tracking concerns.

Who can submit a concern? ▼
Any currently enrolled student of the university can submit a concern. You do not need to log in — simply fill out the form on the home page. Faculty and staff concerns are handled through a separate internal channel.
Is it truly anonymous? ▼
Yes. When the "Submit Anonymously" toggle is enabled, no personal data is collected or stored. Not even our administrators can identify who submitted an anonymous concern. However, please note that fully anonymous concerns may be harder to follow up on if we need additional information.
How long does it take for my concern to be addressed? ▼
Our team reviews all submissions within 24 hours and forwards them to the appropriate department. Simple facility issues are often resolved within 48–72 hours. More complex matters (academic disputes, policy changes) may take 1–2 weeks. You'll receive email updates if you submitted with your contact information.
Can I submit the same concern multiple times? ▼
We encourage you to submit a concern only once to avoid duplicate entries. If your original concern has not been addressed after 5 business days, you may submit a follow-up using the same category and referencing your original concern in the description.
What happens to resolved complaints? ▼
Resolved complaints are archived in our system for 2 years for record-keeping and accountability purposes. They are used to track patterns of recurring issues and improve campus services over time.
Can I withdraw or edit my concern after submitting? ▼
Once submitted, concerns cannot be edited directly by students. If you need to withdraw or correct a submission, please contact us at [email protected] with the details of your submission (title, date, category). Anonymous submissions cannot be withdrawn.
What kinds of concerns are NOT accepted? ▼
We do not accept complaints targeting specific individuals by name for personal grievances, submissions that contain hate speech or threats, or requests for academic grade changes (which must go through the Registrar). Emergency situations should be reported directly to Campus Security at ext. 911.
How do I know if my concern was received? ▼
After submitting, a confirmation pop-up will appear on screen. If you provided your email address, you will also receive a confirmation email within a few minutes. If you submitted anonymously, please take note of the category and date in case you need to follow up.

📋 Terms of Use

Last updated: January 1, 2026

1. Acceptable Use

By using Campus Voice, you agree to submit concerns in good faith and for legitimate campus improvement purposes only. This platform exists to bridge students and administration — please use it responsibly.

2. Prohibited Content

You must not submit false, misleading, or malicious reports. This includes fabricated incidents, targeted harassment of staff or students, or spam submissions. Misuse of the platform may result in disciplinary action under UC's student code of conduct.

3. Content Review

All submissions are subject to review by our moderation team. Campus Voice reserves the right to remove or withhold content that violates these terms or is deemed inappropriate, off-topic, or harmful.

4. Anonymous Submissions

While anonymous submissions are protected, they are still subject to these terms. Anonymity does not grant permission to submit malicious content.

5. Changes to Terms

These terms may be updated periodically. Continued use of the platform constitutes acceptance of the most current version.
